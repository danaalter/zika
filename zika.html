What is Zika, and why is it so serious?

The Zika virus is a flavivirus, and is part of the same family as yellow fever and West Nile. BUT, unlike some of those viruses, there is NO vaccine to prevent Zika, or any known medicine to treat the infection. Zika is commanding attention because of an alarming connection between the virus and microcephaly, a neurological disorder that results in babies being born with abnormally small heads. It causes severe developmental issues and sometimes death.
In most people, symptoms of the virus are mild, including fever, headache, rash and possible pink eye. In fact, 80% of those infected never know they have the disease.
